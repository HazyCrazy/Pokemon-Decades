During New Game, there is a Funbro Trap, made with intent to fuck with Speed Up Spamers.
You can avoid this trap by simply chooseing the "I decide how I use it" Option, during the Delta Speed Up question.
The Funbro trap is intentionally designed to be unwinnable, and unlosable. 

Pokemon Decades is not designed for Nuzlockes and has free form Level Caps that the player manually enforces themselves. 
Key Items such as the Auto Win Policy and No Clip Disk are intended parts of the Trainer Suite, and are not Debug Items.

The Fast Travel Phone's Sequence Skip Option is an intended part of the Trainer Suite, and is not a Debug Item.
It exists to allow the player to bypass parts of the game they are bored with, or don't care about.
Sometimes, you just want to play a hyper specfic part of a video game. 
And when you just want that, the idea of grinding through playtime hours you don't care about,
is enough to kill all desire to play a video game. And it saves you time, if you want to show something to your friends.

The Basic Vanilla Gen 9 Patch is unfinished. The minimum patches are finished,
but Patches 5 to 8 are not done, due to burnout in the lead up to Decade's Beta release.

The Item and PSS Suites exist for 2 purposes:

1. Make the workload of a full region game feasible for a one person development team (The 10 month turnaround of Decades's development would not have been possible without these suites)
2. Ensure that the time spent doing a challenge run is spent on engaging with the challenge run, and not the tedious setup to start a challenge run that adds up over hundreds of attempts (Radical Red in-game cheats, Elite Redex's early game slog, the slog of moving past the repeating welcome to the world of Pokemon and getting the Pokedex New Game start)

Due to the open ended nature of Decade's design, I cannot ensure a strict Monotype Hardcore Nuzlocke is feasible.

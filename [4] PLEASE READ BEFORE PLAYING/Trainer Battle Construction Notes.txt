Trainer Setup Notes

Nearly every Pokemon in Super Yellow always have 1 Regular Ability.
The rest of a Pokemon's 5 Abilities are all Hidden Abilities.
The Ability Vial Key Item allows for full unconditional Ability changing.

Nearly all Pokemon have just 1 Regular Ability, for the purposes of removing the tedium of generating a Pokemon with it's general best ability.

In Super Yellow, all moves affected by Friendship have been tweaked or overhauled, to remove min/maxing of the Friendship value.
Return is a simple 110 Base Power, 85% Accuracy Fairy Type move, Frustration is a simple 110 Base Power, 85% Accuracy Dark Type move. 
Egg Bomb is the new and Auto Category Changing Return.

BaseMoney sets the default value of SkillLevel for every trainer.
It is best if possible, to manually set the SkillLevel in a Trainer Battle event.
BaseMoney is needed to manage the pace of the player obtaining resources.

All Trainer Battles in Super Yellow are divided into Triple, Double and Single battles, for every instance of a Trainer Battle Event.
This has to be done, because the player can freely change their Battle Format Preference, and is not locked into a Battle Format. 
All Trainer Battles need at least 3 Pokemon, in order for the player to not get confused when setting their Battle Format preference. 

Auto Level Scaling is required, in order to cut the amount of battles for each trainers needed from a minimum of 12, to just 3.
A consequence of this, is that Little Cup teams cannot be fully optimized. 
Lower Level's fundementally change nuances. Leftovers sucks in Little Cup, and the Berry Juice becomes the de facto Healing Held Item. 
Berry Juice is worthless junk outside Little Cup, and Leftovers becomes the de facto Healing Held Item.
You will have to use the Honey Item (33% max HP recovery), to act as a compromise.
You also don't be able to give foes the Eviolite item, because auto scaling means a Pokemon's Evolution stage can change at the drop of a hat.
The Eviolite only works on Pokemon that are not fully evolved.

Battle Gimmick Notes 

When the Map is setup correctly, All 4 Battle Gimmicks, Gen 6 to Gen 9, can be present all at once. 
The order of gimmicks is Mega Evolution, Z-Moves, Dynamax, and Tera. 
A single Pokémon cannot use all of them at once, but any Pokémon that meets what is required to 
Dynamax or Tera can use them one after another. This means once Dynamax wears off, a Pokémon can Tera straight after.
When Megas and Z-Moves are used, you cannot also Dynamax or Tera straight after.
Super Yellow has the Dynamax Inhibitor Item, in order for the player to use Dynamax alongside Megas, Z-Moves and Tera.

All Trainer Battles in Battle Mode, must be crafted with a Mega Captain, Z-Move Captain, Tera Captain and Dynamax Captain,
if a Trainer's Pokemon team is able to squeeze all 4 gimmicks into a team composition.
This must be done, because the player and Battle Mode opponent have equal unlimited access to all the game's items.
If the player is on paper, able to craft a team that uses all 4 Battle Gimmicks. 
The opponent also has to have a team with all 4 Battle Gimmicks. 

Otherwise, the player can easily beat any team 
with Dynamax and Z-Move abuse. Mainly Dynamax abuse in Singles. Up to you if Dynamax is banned in a Single battle.
It's a mechanic that can only work in Double/Triple battles. It otherwise breaks the game's balance. 
But from my time testing/playing in all Battle Formats. 
I've grown to like Dynamax, mainly because of the instant Defense response it brings, in all formats.
But Defense play doesn't matter in Singles, when Dynamax Moxie Gyarados exists. 
The best gimmick metagame is Teras only. But I like the sandbox of all 4 battle gimmicks present in a battle.

With the Tera mechanic, Shedinja's Tera Type is set to Stellar.
This is required, to stop Air Balloon Electric Type Shedinja from breaking the game, unless Electric types are weak to Rock types.

In order for the Metagame Wiki dex in game, to do it's job of making all Ability and Move changes be documented,
without the headache of maintaining a online wiki. The Pokedex Data Page is used, and  
Arceus can learn every Ability and Move in the game.
20 Pokemon of a specfic Type, can learn every Move that matches one of their Types, in order for all Move changes to be easily searchable in game.
9 to 10 Pokemon can learn every Move in the game. For the purposes of removing all issues with Legal Move Sets via Breeding Chains.
Ditto and Smeargle can breed with any Pokemon that does not have the Legendary/Mytical Flag. (Refer to pokemon.txt)
They are both 2 of the 9 to 10 Pokemon that learn every Move in the game.
Ultimate Move Tutor is configured to allow the Breeding and TM mechanics to be removed from Super Yellow. 
And The All Moves Disk in the Cheats Pocket, toggles allowing any Pokemon to learn any Move in the game.
This is to remove all error in linking the whole history of every Pokemon's Evolution Family line's Learn Sets.
The player can choose for themselves, if they want to abuse the All Moves Disk.
Ultimate Move Tutor also Applies Universal Moves and Universal Type Moves, for all Pokemon.
Starters, labled as VGC Starters, can learn nearly every move that makes a Pokemon viable to great in Double/Triple battles.
This is to make sure whatever team the player makes, they can use the whole way through, 
the starter they will get attached to in any variation of investment.

Opponent Pokemon EV Allowcation Notes 

Due to the complex formula EV Allocator uses to auto adapt opponent Pokemon EV's,
it is not possible for foes to have complex EV Spreads in pratice. 

All opponent Pokemon need 256/256 EV Spreads, in order for the assigned EV's to be applied correctly.
Opponent Pokemon due to the EV Allowcator Formula and Level Scaling, cannot reach the max EV Limit in pratice.
Opponent Pokemon from Lvl. 5 to Lvl. 100 can have 240 EV's max in each stat. Being able to max out up to 2 stats only. 
Sunshine Yellow has modded the EV Allocator to allow Lvl.5 Pokemon to get 256 EV's max in a stat.

This indirectly gives the player an easier time against all foes.

Story Mode Trainers 

The Key parts of making a Story Mode trainer:

They are not required to be fully optimized. Because they exist as level pacing tools,
it's bad to make them fully optimised, if they are just a common trainer.

Pokemon SY is going to use 1:1 Level Scaling in the same manner that Elite Redux does it.
Simply because it's too much of a technical hassle to set up Difficulty Options, that does not involve Events.
I want anything that is an option to use Key Items as part of a trainer's suite, if it's possible.  
I hate going to a specfic location in any Pokemon game to change options. I want options to always be Key Items or Menu Options selections. 

Story Mode Trainers only need 1 entry, compared to Battle Mode Trainers.
Story Mode trainers will always need at least 3 Pokemon, due to the player being able to easily change Battle Formats in the Options Menu.
It is up to the player to decide what Battle Format they prefer, and stick to it. 

Story Mode Trainer Pokemon will require Movesets to be manually applied. 
Story Mode Trainer Pokemon automatially have 0 IV's, and EV's scale based on Level, up to 85 in all stats. 
Common Story Mode Trainers are not forced battles. The player can always say no to a battle, if the context of the battle does not require the fight to be mandatory.
The player can choose if the battle will be a regular battle with Exp Gain, or a Metagame fight, that allows the player to forfeit the battle, at the cost of not gaining Exp. 
I had no luck finding a resource that works, that tweaks normal battles where you can always quit the fight, and are not locked into a fight.  

Story Mode Type Masters/Gym Leaders

The Key parts of making a Story Mode Type Master/Gym Leader:

Type Masters will have 2 versions of their teams.

The first version is their Full Power Team. This is their intented team, straight out of Battle Mode. 

The second version is their Gym Challenge Teams. This is 10 variations of their teams, based on how the offical games pace the challenge of the Gym Leaders/Elite Four/Champion. 
With a sprinking of how Renegade Platinum handled the Gym Leaders, to make for fun and engaging, but not sweat drenching fights.
The offical games generally have Gyms 1 to 3 have 3 Pokemon. Gyms 4 to 6 have 4 Pokemon. Gyms 7 to 8 have 5 Pokemon. Elite 4 have 5 Pokemon on their teams, and the Champion has a full 6 Pokemon team. 
This option takes it's cue from Tectonic, and to stop the problem that happens with games such as Elite Redux and Radical Red, where the hardcore intented experince, can become a stone wall 
that stops the player from playing the game. 

While a major pain in the ass, this is the consequence of making a non linear Pokemon game. I do not need to make 18 variations of each Type Master. After Gym 10, just use the Battle Mode teams.

Both Trainers and Wild Pokemon are Level Scaled. This is done to make the workload as easy as possible. And the very existance of the Doom and Level Candies means I need Level Scaling anyway. 
Because I'll be honest, I like Pokemon the most with 1:1 Level Scaling. That is how the Metagame of Pokemon is played after all.

Wild Pokemon also follow the same Level Scaling system as the trainers of Story Mode.
